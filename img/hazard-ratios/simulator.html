SSA 2023 Male period life table, ages 0–99. Draw a hazard ratio curve to see how it translates to life expectancy.

μ(t) — Baseline Hazard Rate
The instantaneous risk of death at age t, conditional on surviving to that age.
Export:
S(t) — Survival Function
The probability of surviving from birth to age t.
Export:
P(t) = μ(t) · S(t) — Death Density
The unconditional probability density of dying at age t. Peaks where hazard is high and enough people are still alive.
Export:
L(t) — Conditional Life Expectancy
Expected remaining years of life at age t.
Export:
P(t) × L(t) — Sensitivity of Life Expectancy to Hazard Changes
The product of death density and remaining life expectancy. This is the weight function in the ΔL integral.
Export:
HR(t) — Draw Your Hazard Ratio Curve
Click and drag to draw. HR = 1 means no change. HR < 1 reduces mortality, HR > 1 increases it.
