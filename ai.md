The following is a candid description of how AI is used on dynomight.net.

All words are written by me (a human) by physically pressing keys with my fingers. All of them. The only exceptions would be quotes from other humans, or something that's clearly labeled as an [AI output](/automated/).

I do often use AI for the following purposes:

1. For research, e.g. "Who discovered vitamin D?"
2. For fact checking, e.g. "Here is a blogposts on vitamin D. Please check for errors."
3. To check for low-level spelling and grammar errors.

For research and fact-checking, I treat AI as a not-very-reliable source. I typically ask for references and verify them. And empirically, I find that AI *is in fact wrong* a huge percentage of the time. That said, for specific factual questions about very well-understood domains (e.g. "What organelle makes ATP in eukaryotes?") I do sometimes judge the answers to be reliable enough to trust. Any mistakes should be considered my mistakes.

I more rarely use AI for the following purposes:

1. As an advanced thesaurus, e.g. "Give me words halfway between 'desperate' and 'tenacious'".
2. For low-level editing, e.g. "This sentence seems long and clunky, give me five other ways it could be re-written."
3. For high-level editing suggestions, e.g. "How would you reorganize this post? (Describe the organization, don't actually rewrite.)"
4. To write code.

On average, I probably do one of these around an average of once per post. In addition, I impose a red line that not a single word can be cut and pasted from AI into a post, ever. (Never, not a single word.) See [this post](https://dynomight.net/blink/) for more thoughts on the use of AI in writing.
